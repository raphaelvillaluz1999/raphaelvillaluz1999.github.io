My work centres on developing numerical frameworks to understand nutrient flux, coastal dynamics, and their implications for marine ecosystems. I am currently building pipelines to simulate coastal processes, with a specific interest on how hydrodynamic models can be coupled with biogeochemical modules to predict large-scale environmental changes. My recent work involved developing a module for Shizugawa Bay, where I programmed the interaction between oyster aquaculture density and plankton bloom dynamics within the Regional Ocean Modeling System (ROMS).

I recognise the intersection of high-performance computing (HPC) and environmental science. My objective is to create comprehensive and reproducible tools that facilitate improvements in coastal management and climate physics research. I am currently open to opportunities that leverage large-scale environmental data and computational modelling.
